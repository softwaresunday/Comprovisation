Findings
  1. Birds landing produced a nice step up in frequency.
  2. Jumpy birds meant that more than one would leave the perch at a time.
  3. Tweets from @budgerigarMLS when birds arrived or left the perch.
  4. Birds canoodling around caused fluxuations in frequency.
  5. Birds near the edge of the cage caused extreme readings.
  6. Needed to compensate for the frequency drift in the cheap theremin design.
  7. After running the system overnight the system was tweeting:
           There are now -120 birds on the perch
    (That's 120 negative birds ...    and the twitter feed now had two followers!).
